Climate Action

What is Climate Action?

Reducing Greenhouse Gas Emissions

Efforts to decrease emissions that contribute to global warming.

Promoting Sustainable Practices

Encouraging eco-friendly practices in daily life and industries.

Enhancing Climate Resilience

Building systems and communities that can withstand climate impacts.

Protecting Natural Ecosystems

Preserving natural habitats to maintain biodiversity.

Encouraging Renewable Energy Use

Promoting the use of clean, renewable energy sources.

Supporting Global Climate Agreements

Participating in international efforts to combat climate change.

What is it For?

Preventing Climate Disasters

Aiming to reduce the occurrence of extreme weather events.

Protecting Public Health and Safety

Minimizing health risks associated with climate change.

Promoting Environmental Justice

Ensuring all communities benefit equally from environmental policies.

Sustaining Natural Resources

Preserving resources for future generations.

Ensuring Economic Stability

Reducing economic risks related to climate change impacts.

Enhancing Global Collaboration

Fostering international cooperation to address climate challenges.

How Does it Work?

Mitigation

Actions to reduce or prevent greenhouse gas emissions.

Adaptation

Adjusting to actual or expected climate changes.

Resilience Building

Enhancing the ability of systems to cope with climate impacts.

Education and Advocacy

Raising awareness and promoting policy changes for climate action.

Partnerships and Collaboration

Working together across sectors to tackle climate change.

Monitoring and Evaluation

Tracking progress, reporting results, and adjusting strategies.
